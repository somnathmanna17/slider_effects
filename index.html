MOOSE
The moose (Alces alces) is the largest member of the deer family, found in northern forests across North America, Europe, and Asia. Known for their long legs and large, flat antlers (on males), moose can weigh up to 1,600 pounds. They are herbivores, feeding on leaves, bark, and aquatic plants, and are strong swimmers that often wade into water for food. Moose are typically solitary and well-suited to cold climates, with thick fur and limbs built for deep snow. Despite their calm appearance, they can be aggressive when threatened, especially mothers with calves. They rely more on smell and hearing than sight and have few natural predators aside from wolves and bears. Human activity and vehicle collisions are major threats to their survival.

ELEPHANT
Elephants are the largest land animals, with three species: African savanna, African forest, and Asian elephants. Known for their trunks, large ears, and tusks (in most males), they use their trunks for breathing, eating, drinking, and communication. African elephants have larger ears, while Asian elephants are smaller with rounded ears. Highly intelligent and social, elephants live in matriarchal herds and display emotions like joy, grief, and empathy. They communicate through sounds, touch, and ground vibrations. Herbivores by nature, they eat grasses, bark, fruit, and roots, consuming large amounts daily. Elephants are vital to their ecosystems, helping shape landscapes and spread plant seeds. Despite their importance, they face major threats from habitat loss, human conflict, and poaching for ivory. Both African and Asian elephants are endangered, with conservation efforts focused on protecting their habitats and stopping illegal trade.

Royal Bengal Tiger
The Royal Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) is a magnificent and powerful big cat native to the Indian subcontinent, mainly found in India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan. It is the most populous tiger subspecies and is easily recognized by its striking orange coat with bold black stripes and a white underbelly. Adult males can weigh up to 500 pounds and are known for their strength, agility, and stealth. Bengal tigers are solitary and territorial animals, primarily hunting at night. Their diet consists mainly of deer, wild boar, and other large herbivores. As apex predators, Bengal tigers play a vital role in maintaining the health and balance of forest ecosystems by regulating prey populations. They inhabit a range of environments, from mangrove swamps and grasslands to tropical forests. Despite their iconic status, they face serious threats from habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. The species is currently listed as endangered, making conservation efforts critical for their survival.
